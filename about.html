Cruising Celiac

About

Website Creator

When I was 11, I was diagnosed with Celiac disease, an autoimmune disorder that makes me allergic to gluten(wheat, rye barley). For years, I suffered as the Indian food I could make and eat outside gave me allergic reactions and could only stay away from the diet entirely. Over time, my family and I figured out what worked, what didn't, and successfully reintroduced South Asian foods into my diet. But many other South Asians with similar gluten intolerances and Celiac disease do not have access to this same support system or any resources about eating South Asian AND gluten free. So, I worked to create this website for my Girl Scout Gold Award Project, hoping to provide people with the support and resources they need.

As a Girl Scout Gold Award Project, this website specifically aims to be a sustainable (long-lasting) resource for gluten-free living. It is the highest award available, and marks the end of the Girl Scout journey for many Scouts. However, this website will remain available for anyone who needs it, and our team plans to continue developing and adding to the resource collection.

Mission

  • Improve diet in people with Celiac
  • Improve quality of life of Celiacs through food
  • Share important resources to be gluten-free successfully

If upon going through this resource collection you have any ideas for improvement or wish to add your own content, feel free to contact cruisingceliac@gmail.com. Cruising Celiac welcomes all contributions.
