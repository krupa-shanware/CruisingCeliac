Chakli (Indian Namkeen Snack)

Ingredients

  • 1 cup roasted gram dal
  • 3 cup rice flour (Bob Red Mill GF Rice Flour Or Vitacost GF Rice Flour
  • ½ tsp chilli powder
  • 2 tbsp black sesame seeds
  • ½ tsp ajwain / carom seeds
  • Salt to taste
  • Hot water (for kneading dough)
  • Oil (for frying)

Directions

In a mixer jar take ¾ cup roasted gram dal and grind to fine powder.

Transfer the roasted gram dal powder to a sieve. Add 3 cups of rice flour and sieve the flour.

Also add ½ tsp chilli powder, 2 tbsp black sesame seeds, ½ tsp ajwain, 1 pinch hing, ¾ tsp salt, and 2 tbsp hot oil. Crumble and mix well, making sure the flour is moist.

Add 1 cup hot water and mix well. Press the flour with your hand making sure the flour is moist. Cover and let sit for 5 minutes. Then mix again.

Add water as required and start to knead the dough. Knead until you have a smooth, soft, and nonsticky dough.

Now take the star mold and fix it to the chakli maker. Grease the chakli maker with some oil (this prevents the dough from sticking to the mold).

Make a cylindrical shape out of dough and place the dough inside the maker. Tighten the lid and start preparing chaklis on the steel plate, or you can use a wet cloth or wax/parchment paper make small spiral shape chaklis by pressing. Seal the ends so that it doesn’t fall apart while deep frying.

Take one chakli at a time and slide it into the hot oil. Flip the chakli and fry on low to medium flame until they turn crispy on both sides. Drain over a paper towel to remove excess oil.

Finally, once cooled, enjoy instant chakli or store in an airtight container for 2 weeks.
