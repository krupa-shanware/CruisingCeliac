Chole Bature

Ingredients

  • GF flour - Combination 1: Mix Bob Red Mill 1:1 flour 2 cup, 1 cup sorghum flour, 1/2 cup almond flour OR Combination 2:1 cup Vitacost GF baking mix, 1 cup Walmart Great value GF flour, 1/2 teaspoon baking soda and xanthan gum each
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 teaspoon hot oil to add in above mix
  • Oil for frying
  • Onion
  • Tomatoes
  • Ginger/Garlic
    • Spices - Chili Powder, Turmeric Powder, Chole Masala
  • Lemon/Tamarind juice

Directions

Chole

To make onion-tomato paste, Grind 1 onion, 1 green chilli, and 1-2 tomatoes in a food processor. Alternatively you can cut and fry the onion in a little oil and when it cools down, grind with tomatoes to make a paste.

In a cooking pan, add oil. And mustard seeds, and when splattered add onion-tomato paste. Fry for 5-10 minutes until it releases oil. Add 1 teaspoon of ginger-garlic paste. This gives a nice flavor to the chole.

Now add a pinch of turmeric, 1 spoon of chili powder, 1 spoon of chole masala, and fry. You can adjust the spices based on level of spiciness you like.

Drain water from chole cans, wash well, and add in pan.

Add salt and cook well, and for a tangy taste add some tamarind or lemon juice.

Bhatura

Take GF flour mix and add salt per taste.

Add 1-2 teaspoon of hot oil into the flour mix. It will give a nice crispiness to Bhatura and help it to not absorb oil when cooking.

Knead flour mix with yogurt, and keep aside for 30 min. Yogurt helps to make bhaturas soft.

Roll Bhatura with a rolling pin, but not too thin. If edges are rough, take a small-medium size circle cutter and cut it into a nice circle shape. Sometimes you can also use a lid cover to cut the dough into a circle shape.

Heat oil for frying, ensuring that oil is enough hot for frying. Fry the Bhaturas.

Serve Chole Bhatura with a salad of onion, tomatoes, cucumber, lemon, etc. Serve hot and enjoy!
