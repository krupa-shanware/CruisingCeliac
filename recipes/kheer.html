Seviyan Kheer (Vermicelli Kheer)

Ingredients

GF Thin Rice Noodles (Thai Kitchen Gluten Free Thin Rice Noodles Or Annie Chun's Thin Rice Noodles) Nuts - ½ cup Cashews,¼ cup Raisins, ¼ cup Almonds, ¼ cup Pista, ¼ cup dates Cardamom Milk, Ghee and Sugar Evaporated Milk or Condensed Milk Can Dry rose petals
  • GF thin rice noodles (Thai Kitchen Gluten Free Thin Rice Noodles Or Annie Chun's Thin Rice Noodles)
  • Nuts - ½ cup cashews, ¼ cup raisins, ¼ cup almonds, ¼ cup pistachios, ¼ cup dates
  • Cardamom
  • Milk
  • Ghee
  • Sugar
  • 1 can of evaporated Milk or condensed Milk
  • Dry rose petals

Directions

Take the thin rice noodles and break into small pieces (use something heavy to pound and break it). Make it around 2 cups.

In a pan, add 2 spoonfulls of ghee. Chop nuts and roast nuts until brown. Take out in a bowl.

Now in a big pan, take 3 spoons of ghee, add the broken rice noodles, and roast until golden brown and there is an aroma to the noodles.

Now add 4 cups of milk. Let boil for 10-20 mins, stirring continuously.

Cook the sevaya (aka noodles) in the hot milk.

Add the can of evaporated or condensed milk, adding sugar as needed. If using sweetened condensed milk, adjust the sugar accordingly.

Add the roasted chopped nuts, and ½ spoon cardamom powder. Continuously still until the mixture thickens.

Serve with a sprinkle of nuts and rose petals.

This is one of the most popular Indian desserts, enjoy your GF version!
