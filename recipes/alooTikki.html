Aloo Tikki

Ingredients

  • 6-8 medium-large potatoes
  • 2-3 GF slices of bread or readymade GF bread crumbs
  • Salt to taste
  • Â½ Cup of cilantro
  • Spices - Chat Masala, Chili powder, Cumin powder, Lemon juice
  • Oil for cooking

Directions

Chole

Boil potatoes in pressure cooker or instant pot, then peel and mash them.

Toast the GF slices of bread slightly and make into breakcrumbs in a grinder.

Mix mashed potatoes with GF bread crumbs, cilantro, salt, spices, lemon juice, and mix everything together.

Take out a ball-sized portion of potato mix and roll on your hand to give a round flat shape. Make medium size tikkies.

Heat pan with cooking oil, oil quantity such that tikkis will be almost half covered in oil.

Shallow fry the tikkies on 1 side. When crisply cooked, flip and shallow fry other side.

Take out tikkies onto paper towels and soak up any extra oil.

Bhatura

Take GF flour mix and add salt per taste.

Add 1-2 teaspoon of hot oil into the flour mix. It will give a nice crispiness to Bhatura and help it to not absorb oil when cooking.

Knead flour mix with yogurt, and keep aside for 30 min. Yogurt helps to make bhaturas soft.

Roll Bhatura with a rolling pin, but not too thin. If edges are rough, take a small-medium size circle cutter and cut it into a nice circle shape. Sometimes you can also use a lid cover to cut the dough into a circle shape.

Heat oil for frying, ensuring that oil is enough hot for frying. Fry the Bhaturas.

Serve hot with tomato ketchup and mint chutney or any other chutney of your choice. Serve with onion, cilantro salad or any other salad.
