Pani Puri

This popular street food doesn’t need any introduction! Potato, onion, chickpeas, and stuffed crispy puris drenched in sour and spicy mint flavored water (pudina pani) easily fills one’s mouth and takes taste buds on a journey of heaven. It is no wonder that it’s a popular street snack in all of India and is known as gol gappa, puchka, pakodi, pani puri, etc. Preparing pudina vala pani and masala for pani poori at home is not only a healthier way of enjoying it, but also allows you to customize it the way you like it.

Going gluten free does not mean you have to give up this popular street food. With GF ingredients, you can very well prepare and enjoy it at home.

Ingredients

Poori:

  • Gluten Free All Purpose Flour (Bob Red Mill 1:1 or other)
  • Gluten Free rice sooji(GF rice cereal acts as a good rice sooji)
  • Salt
  • Oil
  • Baking Soda

Pani:

  • Mint
  • Cilantro
  • Chaat Masala
  • Salt
  • Dates
  • Chillies
  • Tamarind

Chutney:

  • Dates
  • Jaggery
  • Tamarind
  • Chilli Powder
  • Cumin Powder

Additional Ingredients: boiled and mashed potatoes, sprouted mung beans, cooked chickpeas, cut onion, and shev(fried snack)

Directions

Puri

Mix 3 cups of all purpose flour with 1 cup of rice sooji and a pinch of baking soda. Add 2 spoons of heated oil, this will help to make the puri crisp. Use warm water and knead into dough.

Roll the dough into a chapati like shape. Take a small circle shaped cookie cutter and cut into small circles.

Heat the oil to frying temperature, add 1 puri, and fry. The thickness of the puri should be so each puri should puff up round like a ball. If the puri does not puff then check the thickness of your puri. Take out fried puri in a big plate lined with paper towels to soak any extra oil.

Similarly roll and fry the rest of the puris, and keep them on side to cool down.

Pani

Mix these into a grinder: 1 cup mint leaves, 2 cups cilantro, 2-3 chillies, juice of 1 lemon, 1 spoon tamarind, 1 spoon chaat masala, cumin powder, and 3-4 dates. Grind to paste.

Take out the mix in a bowl and add water to make it a liquid. Taste should be tangy, spicy and sweet.

Adjust spice with chillies as per your taste.

Sweet Chutney

In a pan, mix 7-10 dates, 4 teaspoons of jaggery, 1/2 cup water, and 2 teaspoons of tamarind. Cook until all is mixed into the consistency of semiliquid

Add salt to taste.

Serve

In a plate, serve a few puris, a glass of pani, and a small cup of sweet chutney.

Take one puri at a time and make a hole in the top. Stuff it with potato, chickpea, onion, moong beans, and top with pani and sweet chutney. Eat the whole puri in one bite and enjoy!
