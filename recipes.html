Recipes

Re-creating South Asian recipes can be tough, especially when you don't know how to adjust for gluten-free ingredients. Here, find recipes that take some thinking to accomodate gluten-free flours and other ingredients. You'll find that simple or naturally GF recipes won't be here, so the foods you've been missing out on will be. Feel free to email us if there are any recipes you'd like to see!

Chole Bhature

Chole Bhature is a delicious meal that will have you making more every week. Made with chickpeas, this meal ranks highly with kids and adults.

Aloo Tikki

Aloo Tikki can be an amazing snack or small meal, and can satisfy anyone's hunger. Though it looks difficult to make, it is truly a simply and amazing recipe that anyone can recreate.

Pani Poori

This popular street food doesn’t need any introduction! Potato, onion, chickpeas, and stuffed crispy puris drenched in sour and spicy mint flavored water (pudina pani) easily fills one’s mouth and takes taste buds on a journey of heaven. It is no wonder that it’s a popular street snack in all of India and is known as gol gappa, puchka, pakodi, pani poori, etc. Preparing pudina vala pani and masala for pani puri at home is not only a healthier way of enjoying it, but also allows you to customize it the way you like it.

Dahi Poori

Dahi Poori is a popular twist of the tradional Pani Poori. It can be consumed as a filling and tasty snack, and is popular among all age groups.

Bhel

Bhel is a savory mixture of various crunchy foods topped with tangy chutnies and vegetables. It is a popular comfort and snacking food that complements tea time. Pair this treat with a steaming cup of chai for ultimate satisfaction.

Saraso ka Saag and Makki ki Roti

This dish is a Northern Indian delicacy made of corn. Due to the abundance of corn fields in India, this dish quickly became popular and is known well to many Northerners.

Shev-Sev Recipe (Indian Namkeen snack)

Sev is a common snack that goes well with other fried snacks such as chakli. It can even be sprinkled on top of some subjis for added crunch!

Chakli (Indian Namkeen Snack)

Chakli is a taste and fun dish to make at home. Made in many parts of India, this dish has some varieties. Follow this recipe to make classic chaklis, but gluten-free!

Seviyan Kheer (Vermicelli Kheer)

Kheer is a heartwarming dish loved by all. Like a cup of hot chocolate or a pudding, kheer delivers the comfort you desire from food.
